What Reedy Stands For

Reedy's priorities are shaped by a decade in the classroom, two years on city council, and a lifetime in the Yakima Valley community he's determined to represent.

Public Safety

Our communities deserve safe streets. Reedy supports fully funding law enforcement, opposing reckless criminal justice rollbacks, and giving local agencies the tools they need to protect families.

Agriculture & Water

The Yakima Valley's orchards and farms are the backbone of our economy. Reedy will fight to protect water rights, push back on overreaching ag regulations, and keep our agricultural heritage strong.

Education & Parent Rights

As a classroom teacher, Reedy knows what students and families actually need. He backs curriculum transparency, local school board control, and policies that empower parents rather than Olympia bureaucrats.

Small Business & Lower Taxes

Yakima's small businesses are the engine of our local economy. Reedy will oppose new tax increases and cut the red tape that makes it harder for Valley entrepreneurs to hire, grow, and compete.

Fiscal Responsibility

Olympia has a spending problem. Reedy will fight for a state budget that lives within its means, holds agencies accountable, and stops growing government at the expense of hardworking Yakima Valley taxpayers.

About Reedy

Your Neighbor.
Your Representative.

"I'm running because LD-15 deserves someone in Olympia who actually lives here, raises kids here, and teaches in our schools."

Reedy Berg grew up in the Yakima Valley, graduated from Eisenhower High School, and earned his degree from Gonzaga University, where he was a collegiate baseball pitcher. After years working in the fruit industry, he chose to give back to the community that raised him by becoming an educator.

Since 2016, Reedy has taught history and civics at Toppenish Middle School. He earned his Master's in Education from Heritage University and brought that same commitment to service onto the Yakima City Council in 2024, where he currently serves as Deputy Mayor.

Reedy and his wife have two young children and family roots in the Valley stretching back more than 40 years. He's not a career politician. He's a teacher, a neighbor, and someone who knows what's at stake for the families of LD-15.
